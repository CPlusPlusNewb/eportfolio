## Scrum Method — Weekly Entry

### What is Scrum?

Scrum is an Agile framework for managing software projects through short, repeatable cycles called **Sprints** (1–4 weeks). Rather than planning everything upfront, teams inspect and adapt continuously to deliver working software incrementally.

---

### The Three Roles

| Role | Responsibility |
|------|---------------|
| **Product Owner** | Manages the Product Backlog; prioritizes features and represents stakeholders |
| **Scrum Master** | Servant-leader who removes blockers and ensures the team follows Scrum |
| **Developers** | Self-organizing team members who build the product Increment each Sprint |

---

### The Sprint Cycle

1. **Sprint Planning** — Team selects backlog items and defines a Sprint Goal
2. **Daily Scrum** — 15-min daily sync to inspect progress and surface blockers
3. **Development Work** — Team builds and tests the Increment
4. **Sprint Review** — Demo to stakeholders; gather feedback
5. **Sprint Retrospective** — Reflect on process; identify improvements

---

### Key Artifacts

- **Product Backlog** — Ordered list of everything the product needs
- **Sprint Backlog** — Items selected for the current Sprint + the plan to deliver them
- **Increment** — The working, shippable output of each Sprint

---

### Reflection

Scrum shifts the mindset from "deliver everything perfectly once" to "deliver something working every Sprint." The most important insight so far is that the ceremonies (especially the Retrospective) aren't overhead — they're how teams actually get better over time.
